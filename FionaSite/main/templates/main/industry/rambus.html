“Cool” Memory Retention Testing

Wang, C.H. et al. Nano Letters 2018 {% endblock %} {% block cardbody %}
DRAM is a promising memory option for quantum computing as it has high retention at low temperatures. The task assigned to me was to determine the exact retention of DRAM at cryogenic temperatures. In order to do so, I worked with teams that designed PCB boards for DDR2/DDR3 in North Carolina to figure out the power requirement of the PCB boards. Afterwards, I set up the testing environment in Sunnyvale for PCB boards and developed software programs for retention testing. I investigated the retention failure mechanism by carefully characterized temperature dependency and writing different patterns. In the end, we determined that GIDL ultimately limited the DRAM retention time and that peripheral circuits need to be customized for low temperature operation.
