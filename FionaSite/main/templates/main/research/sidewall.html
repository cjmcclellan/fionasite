Sidewall/Vertical Growth of MoS2

{# McClellan, IEEE DRC 2017#} {% endblock %} {% block cardbody %}
I'm very excited about this project as it shows a major difference between 2D materials (such as MoS2) and bulk materials like Si and InGaAs. The Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM) image on the right displays monolayer (0.6 nm thick!) Mo2 deposited directly on SiO2/Si sidewall. This could have huge implications for technologies like 3D NAND flash and DRAM, which require high-density transistors (i.e. vertical) for scaling and reducing costs.
